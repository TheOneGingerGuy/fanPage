The Hunger Games

The Hunger Games|Book #1

Katniss Everdeen lives in impoverished District 12, where the Capitol forces each district to send two teenagers to fight to the death in the annual Hunger Games. When her sister Prim is chosen, Katniss volunteers in her place. Alongside fellow tribute Peeta Mellark, Katniss survives the brutal arena using her hunting skills and sharp instincts. Their apparent romance wins public favor, and by threatening joint suicide, they force the Capitol to allow two winners—an act that unknowingly sparks rebellion.

Catching Fire|Book #2

Katniss and Peeta’s defiance turns them into symbols of unrest across Panem. To suppress rebellion, President Snow announces a special Quarter Quell, forcing past victors to compete again. Katniss is thrown back into the arena, where she discovers alliances and a hidden rebel plot. The Games end in chaos when the arena is destroyed, Katniss is rescued by rebels, and she learns that District 12 has been obliterated.

The Mockingjay|Book #3

Now in District 13, Katniss becomes the “Mockingjay,” the face of the rebellion against the Capitol. As propaganda and war escalate, Katniss struggles with trauma, manipulation, and loss. The rebels eventually overthrow the Capitol, but Katniss kills President Coin instead of Snow, rejecting another cycle of tyranny. Panem begins rebuilding, and Katniss slowly heals, forming a quiet future with Peeta.

Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes|Book #4

Set decades earlier, this novel follows a young Coriolanus Snow before he becomes president. Assigned as a mentor in the 10th Hunger Games, Snow works with tribute Lucy Gray Baird from District 12. As he navigates ambition, love, and survival, Snow’s moral decay becomes clear. The story reveals how the Hunger Games evolved and how Snow’s hunger for power shaped Panem’s cruelty.

Sunrise on the Reaping|Book #5

Set during the Second Quarter Quell, this novel centers on Haymitch Abernathy, District 12’s eventual mentor. Forced into a Games with twice the number of tributes, Haymitch survives through intelligence and defiance rather than brute strength. His victory comes at immense personal cost, revealing how the Capitol deliberately breaks victors to maintain control and deepening the tragic history behind Katniss’s world.
